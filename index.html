Executive summary

A positive early read on the new Whangarei layout, strongest for high-intent users

Across two matched 19-day windows, before and after the new layout went live on 5 June, Whangarei increased enquiries from 7 to 12 key events, a 71% rise, while active users grew from 102 to 793. That is a positive directional read for the new layout.

The overall engagement rate fell from 97.3% to 32.2%. This is mainly a traffic-mix effect: Meta paid-social made up about 86% of post-launch users and brought a large volume of lower-intent visitors. When Meta is separated out, non-Meta traffic engaged at 74.0% and produced all 12 key events, an 11.0% enquiry rate.

Read this as a direction of travel, not statistical proof. The traffic mix changed significantly between the two periods, so the comparison should be interpreted with that caveat. Fixing the tracking gaps noted later will make the next read more conclusive.

09
What to do next

Five recommendations

Proceed with the new layout on a directional basis, close the tracking gaps so the next read is conclusive, and treat paid-social traffic differently from high-intent traffic.

Tracking gap to fix first. Phone clicks, email clicks, directions and maps clicks, main CTA clicks and review clicks are not properly captured today. Until they are, every enquiry measure in this report is a floor, not a true total. The first four recommendations below build directly on closing this gap.
1
Proceed directionally with the new layout
The matched-period read is positive, so the direction is sound. Hold off on a confident, full rollout decision until tracking is complete and a cleaner window, with a more stable traffic mix, can be measured.
2
Fix GTM tracking for the key interactions
Tag phone clicks, email clicks, directions and maps clicks, main CTA clicks and review clicks in Google Tag Manager. These are the missing business actions that the dealer page is built to drive, and they are needed before the next read can be conclusive.
3
Confirm the June 6 form submissions are unique enquiries
Six submissions from one source on a single day account for half the post-launch key events. Verify they are six separate enquiries rather than repeats or a test before the lead count is reported.
4
Add reviews and social proof higher on the page
Review clicks are already happening from non-Meta visitors. Surfacing reviews and social proof earlier should reinforce trust while users are still deciding.
5
Test sticky mobile CTAs for Phone, Email and Enquire
Mobile is the leading device for engaged non-Meta users. Persistent, easy-to-reach contact actions on mobile should lift completed enquiries, and the new tracking will measure the effect.
6
Create a shorter mobile-first landing page for Meta traffic
Instead of sending paid-social users to the full dealer page, route them to a focused, mobile-first landing page built for that intent. This is the most direct way to address the volume-without-enquiries problem from Meta.

The bottom line

Conclusion

Directional read

A positive early read on the new Whangarei layout, strongest for high-intent users

Across two matched 19-day windows, before and after the relaunch on 5 June, Whangarei increased enquiries from 7 to 12 key events, a 71% rise, while active users grew from 102 to 793. Non-Meta visitors engaged at 74.0% and produced all 12 key events, and the form completed at 70.5% once started. Together that is a positive directional read for the new layout.

The result should be read with care. The overall engagement rate fell from 97.3% to 32.2%, and views per user and average engagement time also dipped, but this is mainly because Meta paid-social brought a large volume of lower-intent traffic, not because the layout performs worse. This is a direction of travel, not statistical proof. The right next step is to fix tracking, treat paid-social traffic on its own, and measure a cleaner window before a full rollout decision.
