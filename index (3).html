01
Executive summary

Whangarei is driving more customer actions. Franklin engages a smaller audience more deeply.

Whangarei, the newer dealer page, is the stronger performer for business actions across the full 90 day period. It drew more active users, more views, more events, and far more key events than Franklin. Over the same window it recorded 38 key events against Franklin's 1.

Franklin tells the opposite story on quality. Its engagement rate and average engagement time are both higher, but its audience is small and it produced almost no key events. The two pages are strong in different ways, so this report keeps those findings separate rather than averaging them into a single score.

The result holds once the traffic mix is taken into account. Looking at organic and paid search only, with Meta paid-social removed, Whangarei still produced 20 search-driven key events to Franklin's 1. The main caveat is that Meta paid-social sent a large volume of Whangarei traffic that converted into no key events, so that channel should be reviewed on its own.

The June traffic spike was caused primarily by the Meta paid-social campaign 2606-northland-agent-landing-page-test, which began driving traffic on 13 June and peaked on 14 June. This campaign created most of Whangarei's recent traffic volume, but it generated 0 key events, so the spike should be read as campaign-driven awareness traffic rather than proven lead-generation traffic.

Campaign drove the spike, but not the leads. Across the last 30 days, Meta paid-social brought 373 active users to Whangarei at a 23.17% engagement rate, and recorded 0 key events. No form submissions, dealer phone clicks or dealer email clicks were tracked from this traffic in the measured period.

On the peak day, 14 June, the campaign generated 108 of 117 active users, roughly 92% of traffic. Because it produced 0 key events, it should be treated as an awareness and traffic driver, not a proven lead-generation driver.

Business drivers

Why Whangarei outperforms Franklin

This is a strategic interpretation of the GA4 patterns above, not a new set of figures. It explains the gap in business outcomes through four observable differences: marketing activity, traffic distribution, conversion design and how those combine.

A

Marketing activity difference

Whangarei
  • Heavy Meta paid-social traffic from Facebook and Instagram campaigns
  • Strong Google organic presence
  • Higher overall campaign investment and visibility
Franklin
  • Mostly organic traffic
  • No significant paid social push
  • No strong acquisition campaigns
Key interpretationWhangarei is actively being pushed into the market while Franklin is not. The 373 users from Meta paid-social, against Franklin's near-zero paid acquisition, is the clearest signal of that difference.
B

Traffic distribution difference

Whangarei traffic mix
  • High-volume Meta paid-social traffic, low conversion quality
  • Strong organic search traffic, high conversion quality
  • Strong direct traffic
Franklin traffic mix
  • Small organic traffic only
  • Limited paid acquisition
  • No scale in acquisition channels
Key interpretationWhangarei has both scale and intent in its traffic. Franklin has only a small amount of high-intent traffic. Scale combined with intent is what produces measurable customer actions.
C

Conversion architecture difference

Whangarei, built to convert
  • Clear calls to action: book a test ride, call, email
  • Multiple contact methods
  • Step-by-step explanation
  • Trust elements: customer reviews, warranty and a personal "Meet Graham" introduction
  • Localised messaging for Northland
Franklin, more informational
  • Less aggressive conversion structure
  • Lower call-to-action density
  • Fewer guided user actions
Key interpretationWhangarei is optimised for lead generation, Franklin is not. The spread of Whangarei key events across form, phone and email maps directly to its multiple contact paths.
D

Outcome impact, where it all connects

The three differences above compound. More traffic on its own would not convert without a page built to capture it, and a strong page on its own would not produce volume without traffic. Whangarei has both, plus a conversion structure to turn visits into actions. That chain is what shows up in the key event totals.

Why Whangarei wins
  • It receives more traffic, especially paid social and organic
  • It has a stronger conversion-focused design
  • It converts visitors into measurable actions: form_submit, phone and email
Where Franklin wins
  • Higher engagement rate
  • Longer time on page
  • But it lacks scale
  • And it lacks conversions
Key interpretationFranklin engages the few visitors it gets, but without scale or a conversion-focused structure that engagement does not turn into leads. Whangarei turns traffic into actions.
E

The correct way to read this result

A single-cause explanation would be misleading. Whangarei's stronger performance should not be attributed to page design alone, because traffic volume and acquisition strategy are doing a large share of the work.

Correct explanationWhangarei's stronger performance is driven by a combination of higher marketing activity, a better traffic acquisition strategy and a stronger conversion-focused page structure. Remove any one of these and the advantage narrows.

What to do next

Seven recommendations

Practical next steps based on the figures above. They keep what is working, fix the channel that is not, and set up a cleaner comparison for the future.

1
Keep refining the newer Whangarei page structure
It is driving significantly more business-value actions than the older Franklin page, so the newer layout is the right base to build on.
2
Do not credit design alone for the difference
Traffic source mix is a major factor here. Meta paid-social pushed high-volume, low-quality traffic to Whangarei, which inflates the raw totals.
3
Review Meta paid-social end to end
Check targeting, creative, landing-page message match, audience intent and campaign objective. The current Meta traffic is not converting.
4
Prioritise high-intent channels
Local organic search, Google organic, direct and paid search are producing stronger engagement and the key events. Put budget and attention there.
5
Investigate why Franklin visitors are so engaged
Franklin may have content, audience intent or source-quality signals worth applying to other dealer pages, even though its volume is low.
6
Track phone, email and form as the success actions
Treat dealer_phone, dealer_email and form_submit as the primary measures of dealer-page performance going forward.
7
Run a cleaner test next time
Apply the newer design to more dealer pages and compare them over the same date range with similar traffic sources, so design effect can be separated from traffic effect.
16
The bottom line

Conclusion

Summary judgement

Whangarei is the stronger performer for customer interaction today

The newer Whangarei dealer page is currently the stronger performer for customer interaction and business-value actions. It produces substantially more form submissions, phone clicks and email clicks than Franklin in both the 90 day and recent 30 day views. Franklin has stronger engagement quality, but its traffic volume is low and it produced no key events in the last 30 days.

The main caveat is traffic mix. Meta paid-social is driving large volumes of low-quality traffic to Whangarei, while organic and direct traffic are producing the strongest outcomes. The right reading is that Whangarei is winning on business actions now, not that the design alone is responsible.

Whangarei's higher traffic volume was heavily influenced by the Meta paid-social campaign 2606-northland-agent-landing-page-test. The campaign explains the sudden 13 June surge and the 14 June peak. Because Meta paid-social generated 0 key events, the strongest business-value interactions came from other channels, especially organic, direct and email traffic. The correct conclusion is that Whangarei is outperforming Franklin on total customer actions, but the traffic spike itself was campaign-driven and not all of that traffic was high quality.
